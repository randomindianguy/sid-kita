How I'd think about it

Generic conversion % won't work — three products convert through different leading indicators.

Kita Capture
API-based · easiest to trial · likely first product most lenders touch
Leading indicator

% of docs through /revalidate by day 14. Low revalidate + flat submission = abandoned. Low revalidate + rising submission = converging.

Conversion-predictive

Per-doc-type extraction confidence × forward-deployed engineer escalation rate. High escalation rate during a pilot is worth investigating — it often signals document types the platform doesn't yet handle natively.

AI Credit Officer
Touches borrowers directly · deepest integration · hardest to unwind
Leading indicator

Borrower response rate stabilization by day 5, per channel (WhatsApp / Viber / SMS / email). No floor by day 5 = channel-market mismatch.

Conversion-predictive

Completion rate by question type. Drop-off concentrating on specific question types tells the lender what to simplify — and tells you which questions to redesign in the prompt library.

AI Underwriter
Visible to the credit team daily · output quality is immediately felt
Leading indicator

Memo-draft-to-credit-officer-edit ratio trending down week-over-week. Up = the model is wrong; down = the model is learning the lender's underwriting voice.

Conversion-predictive

Per-section override frequency. Repeated overrides on the same section (e.g. income normalization for self-employed) is a feature priority signal, not a model failure.
